\section{Overview}

This report is the reader-facing running record for the synthetic-RLVL formal-logic
chain-of-thought project. Operational handoff files remain in \repo{}, while this
repo keeps the polished narrative, selected tables, selected figures, current
system summaries, and short experiment logs.

The main questions are whether formal-logic CoT is a better reasoning substrate
than natural-language CoT, whether hybrid traces help, and whether formal traces
improve depth extrapolation and downstream transfer under SFT or midtraining.

\paragraph{Current headline.}
The main OLMo-7B HFSA 3-seed depth-scaling grid is complete. Logic is more
depth- and sample-efficient at intermediate train ranges; natural-language
exact traces catch up at the largest train range on joint validity. At
train depth 1--25, depth-50 joint@16 is currently similar: logic 0.417 and
NL 0.427.

\paragraph{Interpretation status.}
The evidence is strongest for synthetic length extrapolation. Downstream
LongBench-style QA runs are useful transfer probes, but current HotpotQA,
2WikiMultiHopQA, and MuSiQue prompts are context-provided answer probes rather
than verified explicit multi-hop-reasoning traces.
